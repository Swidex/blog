/OVERVIEW

Tome is a solution to the common “Last Mile” problem. Our solution directly addresses the problem of patients and other consumers from traveling between their home and their destination. While many are able to walk a mile from their home to their destination, some might struggle with walking or driving to their location. Our solution is a service that takes automated mini-vehicles to transport the consumer from point A to point B.

/ROLE

As the head developer for Tome, my job was working with others to develop facets of the application. We used an agile development cycle. I helped focus our idea down into features of our product. I worked as a Full-Stack developer, working on both the front-end using the Ionic Framework with Angular and the backend API in Javascript. Part of my role as Head Developer was to communicate with the physical robotics team. I worked with our robotics team to communicate our back-end server with our mobile vehicles. Additionally, I created all the promotional material and graphic design for our team's product.

FINDining is a revolutionary new app that solves the problem of not knowing where to eat and not being able to find new, local restaurants. FINDining's advanced restaurant recommendation algorithm uses your eating preferences and tastes in order to give you unique restaurant recommendations. FINDining can also be used socially. Add your friends within the app to see what restaurants they liked and ate at. Get together with a group of friends to create a group within the app and receive restaurant recommendations for the entire group.

/ROLE

Working as a Jr. Developer, I helped develop features for both the back-end API and front-end. For the back-end, I helped develop and improve the back-end API and algorithm for recommending restaurants. For the front-end, I helped creating the aesthetics of the app whilst working the graphic design team to create a pleasing UX and UI.

/OVERVIEW

CyberPatriot is the National Youth Cyber Education Program created by the Air Force Association to inspire K-12 students toward careers in cybersecurity or other science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) disciplines critical to our nation's future. ​At the core of the program is the National Youth Cyber Defense Competition, the nation's largest cyber defense competition that puts high school and middle school students in charge of securing virtual networks.

/ROLE

Starting in 2016, I started learning basic security roles on all systems, including Ubuntu, Debian, Windows, and Windows Server. I also learned about securing networks using Cisco's Packet Tracer. In the following years, I learned more indepth security practices involving multiple Linux distrobutions and services like SSH, FTP, and Samba. In my upperclassmen years, I became the captain of the JV team and eventually Varsity team. I became the club captain and coordinated with National Orginizations to visit large cybersecurity conferences and competitions. In my time as a member of CyberPatriots, we took first in state for Nebraska, and 67th nationally.
